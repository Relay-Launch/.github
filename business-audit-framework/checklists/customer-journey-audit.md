# Customer Journey Audit Checklist

**Relay▸Launch Business Audit Framework**

This checklist evaluates the entire lifecycle of a customer's experience with the business — from the moment they first become aware it exists through becoming a loyal advocate who refers others. Most small businesses focus almost exclusively on the purchase stage and neglect everything before and after it. The result is a leaky funnel at the top, a rough experience in the middle, and zero momentum at the end. This audit maps the full journey and identifies where customers are being lost, frustrated, or underserved.

## How to Use This Checklist

Score each item from 1 to 5 using the scoring rubric in `../scoring/scoring-rubric.md`. For the best results, walk through this audit as if you were a prospective customer. Google the business. Visit the website. Fill out the contact form. Read the onboarding email. Experience what the customer experiences. The owner's perception of the journey and the customer's actual experience are almost never the same.

## Interpreting Results

- **4.0 – 5.0**: The customer journey is a strategic asset. Customers have a consistently excellent experience from first contact through advocacy. Focus on scaling and replicating.
- **3.0 – 3.9**: The journey works but has friction points. Customers get through it but some stages are weak, causing lost prospects, rough onboardings, or silent churn.
- **2.0 – 2.9**: The customer journey is fragmented. Different customers have significantly different experiences, and the business is losing people at multiple stages.
- **1.0 – 1.9**: There is no intentional customer journey. Interactions are reactive, inconsistent, and largely dependent on individual effort rather than systems.

## Journey Mapping Exercise

After completing the scoring, create a visual journey map by answering these questions for each stage:

1. **What does the customer DO at this stage?** (Actions they take)
2. **What does the customer THINK at this stage?** (Questions, concerns, evaluations)
3. **What does the customer FEEL at this stage?** (Emotions — excitement, anxiety, confusion, confidence)
4. **What does the business DO at this stage?** (Touchpoints, communications, actions)
5. **Where is the GAP between expectation and reality?** (Disconnect between what the customer expects and what they experience)

This exercise, combined with the scored checklist, produces an actionable roadmap for improving the end-to-end customer experience.

## Common Findings and Quick Wins

1. **No follow-up sequence for unconverted leads** — Build a 5-email nurture sequence for prospects who inquire but do not buy. This alone can recover 10-20% of lost leads.
2. **Onboarding is improvised** — Document the onboarding process once. Turn it into a checklist. Use it for every new customer. Consistency builds trust.
3. **No review request system** — Send a templated email requesting a Google review after every successful project delivery. Time it to coincide with peak satisfaction.
4. **Customers are not asked for referrals** — Add a referral ask to the 90-day check-in. Happy customers will refer if asked directly and given an easy way to do it.
5. **Slow inquiry response time** — Set up an auto-response that acknowledges inquiries within 5 minutes and commits to a personal response within 4 hours. Speed wins deals.
6. **No progress reporting** — Start sending a monthly or bi-weekly status update to active clients, even if brief. Customers who feel informed are customers who stay.
