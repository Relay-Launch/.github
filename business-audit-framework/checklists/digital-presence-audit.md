# Digital Presence Audit Checklist

**Relay▸Launch Business Audit Framework**

This checklist evaluates how a business shows up in the digital world — its website, search visibility, social media, brand consistency, content strategy, and online reputation. For most small businesses, digital presence is the first point of contact with prospective customers. A weak digital presence does not just miss opportunities; it actively erodes trust. Someone who finds a broken website, an abandoned social media profile, or a string of unanswered Google reviews is making a judgment about the business before they ever speak to a human.

## How to Use This Checklist

Score each item from 1 to 5 using the scoring rubric in `../scoring/scoring-rubric.md`. Conduct this audit with the business's actual digital properties open in front of you. Do not rely on the owner's description of their website — look at the website. Do not take their word on SEO performance — check the data.

## Interpreting Results

- **4.0 – 5.0**: Digital presence is a growth engine. The business is attracting and converting prospects online effectively. Optimize and scale.
- **3.0 – 3.9**: Digital presence is functional but not competitive. Specific improvements will yield measurable increases in traffic, leads, and conversions.
- **2.0 – 2.9**: Digital presence is a liability. Prospects are finding competitors instead, or finding this business and being unimpressed. Significant overhaul needed.
- **1.0 – 1.9**: Digital presence is nearly absent or actively damaging. This is the equivalent of having a storefront with no sign and a broken window. Foundational work required immediately.

## Common Findings and Quick Wins

1. **Google Business Profile is incomplete** — Fill out every field, add 10+ photos, and respond to all reviews. This alone can improve local search visibility within weeks.
2. **Website has no clear CTA** — Add a specific, benefit-driven call to action above the fold on every page. "Get a Free 30-Minute Strategy Session" outperforms "Contact Us."
3. **Social media is inconsistent** — Cut down to one platform and post three times per week consistently rather than posting daily for a week and disappearing for a month.
4. **No review generation system** — Create a simple post-service email with a direct link to your Google review page. Automate it.
5. **Brand is inconsistent across platforms** — Spend two hours updating all profiles to match current branding. This is low effort with high trust impact.
