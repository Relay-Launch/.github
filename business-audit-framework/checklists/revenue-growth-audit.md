# Revenue and Growth Audit Checklist

**Relay▸Launch Business Audit Framework**

This checklist evaluates the financial engine of a business — how money comes in, whether it is priced correctly, how much it costs to acquire and retain customers, and where the realistic opportunities for growth exist. Many small businesses confuse revenue with health. A business can grow revenue while simultaneously becoming less profitable, more fragile, and more dependent on a shrinking number of clients. This audit separates real growth from dangerous growth.

## How to Use This Checklist

Score each item from 1 to 5 using the scoring rubric in `../scoring/scoring-rubric.md`. This audit requires access to financial data. At minimum, you need 12 months of revenue figures, a breakdown by revenue source, customer count and retention data, and marketing spend. If the business cannot produce this data, that finding itself is worth noting — it indicates a measurement gap that makes strategic decision-making nearly impossible.

## Interpreting Results

- **4.0 – 5.0**: The business has a strong financial engine with diversified revenue, healthy margins, efficient acquisition, and strong retention. Focus on scaling and optimizing.
- **3.0 – 3.9**: Revenue is growing but has structural vulnerabilities — concentration risk, underpricing, or leaky retention. Address these before aggressively scaling.
- **2.0 – 2.9**: The financial model has serious gaps. The business may be growing revenue while losing margin, or retaining customers through inertia rather than value. Strategic restructuring needed.
- **1.0 – 1.9**: The business has a revenue problem that threatens viability. Immediate focus on stabilizing revenue, understanding unit economics, and fixing pricing.

## Critical Ratios to Calculate

| Metric | Formula | Healthy Benchmark |
|--------|---------|-------------------|
| LTV:CAC Ratio | Lifetime Value / Customer Acquisition Cost | 3:1 or higher |
| Gross Margin | (Revenue - Direct Costs) / Revenue | 50%+ for services, 30%+ for products |
| Revenue Concentration | Largest Client Revenue / Total Revenue | Below 25% |
| Recurring Revenue % | Recurring Revenue / Total Revenue | 30%+ is ideal for stability |
| Client Retention Rate | Clients Retained / Total Clients (12-month period) | 80%+ |
| Revenue Per Employee | Total Revenue / FTE Count | Varies by industry; track trend |
| Marketing ROI | (Revenue from Marketing - Marketing Cost) / Marketing Cost | 5:1 or higher |

## Common Findings and Quick Wins

1. **Prices have not been raised in years** — Implement a 10-15% price increase for new clients immediately. Existing clients can be raised at renewal with 60 days notice and added value.
2. **No recurring revenue** — Package an existing service as a monthly retainer. Even converting 5 clients to retainers creates baseline predictability.
3. **CAC is unknown** — Add UTM tracking to all marketing links and ask every new client "How did you hear about us?" Start tracking this week.
4. **Client concentration is dangerous** — If one client is 30%+ of revenue, begin diversification immediately. This is an existential risk.
5. **No upsell path** — Map your services from entry-level to premium. Create a natural progression that increases LTV without requiring new customer acquisition.
