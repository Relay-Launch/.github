## Purpose

This procedure defines how the company handles every inbound lead from first contact through a signed engagement agreement. It ensures no lead falls through the cracks, every prospective client receives a professional and consistent experience, and the team qualifies opportunities efficiently so time is spent on prospects who are a genuine fit. A disciplined intake process directly impacts close rates, client satisfaction, and revenue predictability.

---

## Scope

**Applies to:** All inbound inquiries -- website contact forms, direct emails to the company or individual team members, phone calls, referrals from existing clients or partners, LinkedIn messages, and social media direct messages.

**Triggers:** Any communication from a prospective client expressing interest in the company's services.

**Exclusions:** Outbound prospecting and cold outreach follow a separate process (SOP-SAL-002). Existing client upsells and expansion conversations are managed through the Account Management process (SOP-SAL-004). RFP responses follow SOP-SAL-005.

---

## Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| **Business Development Lead (BDL)** | Owns the intake pipeline. Qualifies leads, conducts discovery calls, drafts and presents proposals, and manages the prospect through to signed agreement. |
| **Operations Manager** | Reviews proposed scope for feasibility and team capacity. Approves resource allocation for new engagements. |
| **Managing Director** | Approves proposals above $15,000 or engagements longer than 6 months. Participates in discovery calls for strategic accounts when requested. |
| **Account Manager** | Receives the handoff after agreement signing. Responsible for client onboarding, kickoff, and ongoing relationship management. |
| **Finance** | Generates invoices per the signed agreement terms. Sets up the client in the billing system. |

Before this process can operate effectively, the following must be in place:

- [ ] CRM system (HubSpot) is configured with the pipeline stages defined in this SOP
- [ ] Intake form on the company website is live, tested, and feeding submissions into the CRM automatically
- [ ] Calendar scheduling tool (Calendly) is configured with the BDL's availability and the correct meeting type for discovery calls
- [ ] Proposal template is current, reviewed, and stored in `Sales > Templates > Proposal Template - Current.docx`

- [ ] Pricing guide is current and approved by the Managing Director (`Sales > Pricing > Current Rate Card.pdf`)
- [ ] Auto-response email for form submissions is configured and tested (see CRM workflow "Intake Auto-Response")

## Procedure

### Step 1: Lead Capture (Day 0)

**Responsible:** BDL (or auto-captured by CRM)
**SLA:** Lead must be logged within 2 business hours of receipt.

1. **Capture the inquiry.** Leads from the website intake form are automatically created in HubSpot as new contacts with a deal in the "New Lead" stage. For leads arriving through other channels (email, phone, referral, LinkedIn, social media), the BDL manually creates a contact and deal in HubSpot within 2 business hours.

2. **Record the following information in the CRM deal record:**
   - Contact name, email address, phone number
   - Company name, website URL, and industry
   - Lead source: Website Form, Email, Phone, Referral (note referrer), LinkedIn, Social Media, Conference/Event, Other
   - Initial description of what they are looking for (use their words, not your interpretation)
   - Any known budget or timeline information
   - Urgency indicator: Low (exploring options), Medium (active project in next 1-3 months), High (immediate need)

3. **Tag the lead source accurately.** This data drives marketing attribution reporting. If the lead was a referral, also create a note linking to the referring client's contact record.

4. **Confirm auto-response was sent.** For website form submissions, verify the automated acknowledgment email was delivered (check the CRM activity timeline). For leads from other channels, proceed to Step 2 -- the personal outreach in Step 2 serves as the acknowledgment.

---

### Step 2: Initial Qualification (Within 24 business hours of receipt)

**Responsible:** BDL
**SLA:** Qualification decision and first outreach must happen within 24 business hours of lead capture.

5. **Review the submission and perform a quick qualification assessment.** Open the deal record and evaluate against the following criteria:

   **Qualification Checklist:**
   - [ ] Is this a legitimate business inquiry? (Not spam, not a vendor pitch, not a job seeker)
   - [ ] Is the prospect's need within our service offerings? (Automation, operations consulting, strategic planning, systems implementation)
   - [ ] Is there a plausible budget fit? (Our minimum engagement is $2,500; if the prospect mentions a budget under $1,000 or the business size suggests they cannot afford our services, flag this)
   - [ ] Is the prospect in a market or industry we serve? (We do not take clients in industries on the exclusion list -- see `Sales > Policies > Industry Exclusions.pdf`)
   - [ ] Is there a real project or need, or is this purely informational?

6. **If the lead is qualified:** Move the deal to the "Qualified" stage in HubSpot. Proceed to Step 3.

7. **If the lead is not qualified:** Send a polite and helpful decline email. Use the "Not a Fit" email template (`Sales > Templates > Decline - Not a Fit.md`). Where possible, suggest an alternative resource or referral. Update the deal stage to "Closed - Disqualified" and log the specific reason (out of scope, budget mismatch, spam, etc.). This data helps refine marketing targeting.

8. **If qualification is unclear:** Proceed to Step 3 with a note that the discovery call should prioritize clarifying fit before diving into scope.

---

### Step 3: Discovery Outreach (Within 24-48 business hours of qualification)

**Responsible:** BDL
**SLA:** Outreach email sent within 48 business hours of qualification.

9. **Send a personalized outreach email.** Do not use a generic template for this email. Reference specifics from the prospect's inquiry. The email should include:
   - A brief acknowledgment of their stated need or challenge (1-2 sentences showing you read what they wrote)
   - A sentence about how the company has helped businesses with similar challenges
   - A clear ask: "I'd like to schedule a 30-minute call to learn more about your situation and see if we can help."
   - A link to the Calendly booking page for the "Discovery Call" meeting type
   - 2-3 specific time slots as alternatives for people who prefer not to use scheduling tools
   - Your full signature with phone number

10. **Update the deal stage** to "Discovery Scheduled" once the prospect books a call.

11. **If no response after 48 business hours,** send a follow-up email. Keep it brief: "I wanted to make sure my previous email did not get buried. I'd love to find 30 minutes to learn more about [specific need they mentioned]. Here's my calendar link if any of these times work."

12. **If no response after 5 business days (total),** send a final follow-up. Acknowledge that timing might not be right. Leave the door open: "If now isn't the right time, no problem at all. I'll check back in a few months unless I hear from you sooner." Update the deal stage to "Closed - No Response." Set a CRM task to re-engage in 90 days.

13. **If the prospect responds but is not ready to schedule,** respect their timeline. Ask when they would like you to follow up. Set a CRM reminder for that date. Move the deal to "Nurture" stage.

---

### Step 4: Discovery Call (30 minutes)

**Responsible:** BDL
**SLA:** Call conducted within 5 business days of scheduling.

14. **Prepare for the call (15 minutes before).** Review:
    - The prospect's CRM record and all notes
    - Their company website, LinkedIn page, and any relevant social media
    - Any previous interactions with your company
    - Whether any current clients are in the same industry (potential case study reference)

15. **Conduct the discovery call using the following structure:**

    | Segment | Duration | Focus |
    |---|---|---|
    | Opening | 3 min | Thank them for their time. Brief introduction: who you are, what the company does, and how this call will work. |
    | Their Story | 15 min | Deep listening. Ask open-ended questions (see below). Take detailed notes. |
    | Alignment | 7 min | Reflect back what you heard. Share relevant experience. Explain which service area might be the best fit and why. |
    | Next Steps | 5 min | Outline the proposal process: what you will send, when they will receive it, and what happens after. |

    **Discovery Questions (adapt to the conversation, do not read these like a script):**
    - Tell me about your business. What do you do and who do you serve?
    - What is the biggest operational challenge you are facing right now?
    - How is this challenge affecting your business? (Revenue, time, team morale, growth?)
    - What have you already tried to address it?
    - What does your current tech stack look like? What tools and systems are you using day-to-day?
    - How many people are on your team, and who would be involved in this project?
    - What does a successful outcome look like for you? If we did this work and it went perfectly, what would be different 6 months from now?
    - Do you have a budget range in mind for this work?
    - What is your timeline? Is there a deadline or event driving urgency?
    - Is there anyone else involved in making this decision?
    - What questions do you have for me?

16. **Immediately after the call (within 1 hour),** update the CRM deal record with:
    - Detailed call notes organized by topic (not a transcript -- a structured summary)
    - Updated deal value estimate based on likely scope
    - Decision-maker(s) identified
    - Timeline and urgency level
    - Any red flags or concerns
    - Confirmed next steps

17. **Check scope feasibility with the Operations Manager.** Send a brief Slack message or email summarizing the prospect's need, estimated scope, and timeline. Ask: "Do we have the capacity to take this on in the proposed timeframe?" Wait for confirmation before drafting the proposal.

18. **Update the deal stage** to "Proposal in Progress."

---

### Step 5: Proposal Development and Delivery (Within 3 business days of the discovery call)

**Responsible:** BDL, with review by Operations Manager
**SLA:** Proposal delivered within 3 business days of the discovery call.

19. **Draft the proposal.** Copy the proposal template (`Sales > Templates > Proposal Template - Current.docx`) and customize it for the prospect. The proposal must include:

    - **Executive Summary:** 2-3 paragraphs summarizing the prospect's situation, challenges, and goals -- written in their language, reflecting what they told you on the discovery call. This section proves you listened.
    - **Recommended Approach:** What you will do, broken into clear phases or workstreams. Each phase should have defined deliverables and a timeline.
    - **Deliverables:** A numbered list of every tangible thing the client will receive.
    - **Timeline:** Start date, milestone dates, and end date. Use a simple table or Gantt-style visual.
    - **Investment:** Total cost, broken down by phase if applicable. Payment schedule (e.g., 50% upfront, 50% at completion; or monthly retainer). What is included and, briefly, what is not included.
    - **About Us:** Brief company overview, relevant experience, and 1-2 short client testimonials or case study references.
    - **Next Steps:** Exactly what happens if they want to move forward -- "Sign the attached agreement, and we will schedule your kickoff call within 5 business days."
    - **Terms:** Validity period for the proposal (typically 30 days). Reference to the full engagement agreement.

20. **Internal review.** Before sending the proposal to the prospect:
    - Operations Manager reviews scope, timeline, and resource allocation for feasibility
    - Managing Director reviews and approves any proposal over $15,000 or with a duration exceeding 6 months
    - Check all numbers, dates, and the prospect's name/company name for accuracy (personalization errors are credibility killers)

21. **Send the proposal.** Email the prospect with the proposal attached as a PDF. The email body should be brief:
    - Thank them again for the discovery conversation
    - Summarize the recommendation in 2-3 sentences
    - Attach the proposal and note the key sections
    - Suggest a 15-minute call to walk through it together if they would find that helpful
    - Provide a clear call to action: "Let me know if you have any questions. If everything looks good, the next step is signing the engagement agreement on page [X]."

22. **Update the deal stage** to "Proposal Sent" and set a follow-up task for 3 business days.

---

### Step 6: Follow-Up and Negotiation

**Responsible:** BDL
**SLA:** Follow-up cadence defined below.

23. **3 business days after sending (if no response):** Send a brief follow-up email. "I wanted to check in on the proposal I sent over. Do you have any questions, or would it be helpful to walk through it together on a quick call?"

24. **7 business days after sending (if no response):** Send a second follow-up. Reference a specific benefit or outcome from the proposal. "I know [specific challenge they mentioned] is costing you [impact they described]. I want to make sure we have a chance to discuss the best path forward. Would any time this week work for a quick call?"

25. **14 business days after sending (if no response):** Send a final outreach. Be direct but respectful. "I want to be mindful of your time. If the timing or approach isn't right, I completely understand. I'll plan to follow up in a few months unless I hear from you sooner. In the meantime, do not hesitate to reach out if anything changes."

26. **If still no response after 14 business days:** Update the deal stage to "Closed - No Response." Set a CRM re-engagement task for 90 days. Log the outcome and any theories about why the deal stalled (this data informs process improvement).

27. **If the prospect responds with questions or negotiation requests:**
    - Respond within 1 business day
    - If scope adjustments are needed, revise the proposal and re-submit (mark the new version clearly, e.g., "Proposal v2")
    - If pricing negotiation is requested, the BDL has authority to adjust by up to 10%. Discounts beyond 10% require Managing Director approval. Never discount without adjusting scope -- reducing the price means reducing the deliverables, not doing the same work for less money.
    - If the prospect needs more time, agree on a specific follow-up date and set a CRM reminder

28. **If the prospect declines:** Thank them sincerely. Ask (briefly, not pushily) if they would share their reason -- this feedback is valuable. Update the deal to "Closed - Lost" with the reason. Set a 6-month re-engagement reminder. Send a "keep in touch" note if appropriate.

---

### Step 7: Agreement Signing and Handoff

**Responsible:** BDL (signing), Account Manager (handoff and kickoff)
**SLA:** Agreement sent within 1 business day of verbal acceptance. Kickoff call scheduled within 5 business days of signed agreement.

29. **Prospect confirms they want to move forward.** Send the engagement agreement for electronic signature (DocuSign). The agreement should be pre-populated with:
    - Client legal name and contact information
    - Scope of work (matching the accepted proposal)
    - Total investment and payment schedule
    - Start date and projected end date
    - Terms and conditions

30. **Once the agreement is signed by both parties:**
    - **Update the CRM deal stage** to "Won - Closed"
    - **Record the deal value, close date, and deal source** for reporting
    - **Notify the team.** Post in the #wins Slack channel: client name, what they engaged us for, and deal value.
    - **Send the Welcome Email.** Use the "New Client Welcome" template (`Sales > Templates > New Client Welcome.md`). Include:
      - Confirmation of the engagement and start date
      - Introduction to their Account Manager (name, email, phone)
      - What to expect next (kickoff call scheduling, onboarding questionnaire)
      - Any pre-work or information you need from them before kickoff
    - **Create the project.** Account Manager creates the project in the project management tool (Asana) using the appropriate project template for the engagement type.

31. **Conduct the internal handoff meeting.** BDL and Account Manager meet (15-30 minutes) to transfer context. The BDL walks through:
    - The client's business, goals, and challenges
    - What was discussed on the discovery call
    - The proposal and what was agreed to
    - Any sensitivities, preferences, or relationship nuances (e.g., "They are very detail-oriented and want weekly written updates" or "The CEO is the decision-maker but the COO is the day-to-day contact")
    - All CRM notes and the signed agreement

32. **Account Manager schedules the kickoff call** with the client within 5 business days of the signed agreement. Client onboarding begins per SOP-OPS-001 (Client Onboarding).

33. **Finance sets up the billing.** Create the client in the accounting system (QuickBooks). Generate the first invoice per the payment schedule in the signed agreement. Send the invoice within 2 business days of the signed agreement.

## Exceptions

| Exception | Condition | Modified Procedure | Approved By |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Referral from an existing client** | A current client directly refers a prospect and makes an introduction | Skip the initial qualification screen (Step 2). Move directly to discovery outreach. The referring client's endorsement serves as pre-qualification. Note the referral source prominently in the CRM. Send a thank-you note to the referring client regardless of outcome. | BDL |
| **Returning client (previous engagement)** | A former client reaches out for new work | Skip the discovery call if we already have a thorough understanding of their business (assess case by case). Review past project notes and any feedback. Go directly to proposal if appropriate. | BDL |
| **Inbound from a strategic target** | A company on the strategic target list reaches out | BDL notifies the Managing Director immediately. Managing Director may choose to participate in the discovery call. Proposal approval is required from the Managing Director regardless of deal size. | Managing Director |
| **Rush engagement** | Client has an urgent need and wants to start within 1 week | Compress the timeline but do not skip steps. Same process, faster execution. Discovery call within 24 hours, proposal within 1 business day, agreement same day if possible. Managing Director approval required to commit resources on short notice. | Managing Director |
| **Pro bono or reduced-fee engagement** | The company decides to take on a client at a reduced rate for strategic, community, or portfolio reasons | Requires Managing Director written approval before any pricing is discussed with the prospect. Document the business rationale in the CRM. Follow all other steps normally. | Managing Director |

## Metrics to Track

| Metric | Target | How Measured | Review Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| First response time (lead to first outreach) | Under 24 business hours | CRM: time between deal creation and first outbound activity | Weekly |
| Lead-to-discovery conversion rate | Greater than 60% | CRM: deals reaching "Discovery Scheduled" / total qualified leads | Monthly |
| Discovery-to-proposal conversion rate | Greater than 75% | CRM: proposals sent / discovery calls completed | Monthly |
| Proposal-to-close win rate | Greater than 40% | CRM: deals won / proposals sent | Monthly |
| Average sales cycle length (lead to signed agreement) | Under 21 calendar days | CRM: average days from deal creation to "Won - Closed" | Monthly |
| Average deal value | Track trend (no fixed target) | CRM: average closed deal value | Quarterly |
| Lead source distribution | No single source above 50% | CRM: deals by source | Quarterly |
| Disqualification rate | Under 25% | CRM: disqualified / total leads | Monthly |

## Definitions & Acronyms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **BDL** | Business Development Lead -- the person responsible for managing the sales pipeline and converting leads to clients. |
| **CRM** | Customer Relationship Management system. The company uses HubSpot (free/starter tier). |
| **Discovery Call** | A 30-minute structured conversation with a qualified prospect to understand their needs and determine whether the company can help. |
| **Deal Stage** | The current status of an opportunity in the CRM pipeline. Stages in order: New Lead, Qualified, Discovery Scheduled, Proposal in Progress, Proposal Sent, Won - Closed, Closed - Disqualified, Closed - No Response, Closed - Lost, Nurture. |
| **SLA** | Service Level Agreement -- in this context, the maximum acceptable timeframe for completing a step. |
| **Strategic Target** | A company identified by the Managing Director as a high-priority prospect due to market positioning, revenue potential, or portfolio value. Maintained in `Sales > Strategy > Target Account List.xlsx`. |
