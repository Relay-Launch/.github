## Purpose

This procedure ensures every new employee has a consistent, thorough onboarding experience from the day they accept an offer through the end of their first 90 days. A structured onboarding process reduces time-to-productivity, improves first-year retention, and guarantees the company meets all legal and compliance requirements for new employment. Without it, onboarding quality depends entirely on which manager happens to be handling it -- and that inconsistency costs real money in ramp time, early attrition, and avoidable mistakes.

---

## Scope

**Applies to:** All new full-time and part-time employees, including both on-site and remote hires.

**Triggers:** A candidate formally accepts a written offer of employment and a start date is confirmed.

**Exclusions:** Independent contractors and temporary agency staff are onboarded using SOP-HR-009 (Contractor Onboarding). Interns follow a modified version documented in SOP-HR-006 (Internship Program). Rehires within the last 12 months follow the streamlined process described in the Exceptions section below.

---

## Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| **Operations Manager** | Owns the end-to-end onboarding process. Coordinates across departments. Ensures every checklist item is completed on schedule. Conducts orientation sessions. |
| **Hiring Manager** | Defines the role-specific training plan. Conducts first-week and milestone check-ins. Selects and briefs the assigned buddy. Sets 30/60/90-day goals. |
| **Office Administrator** | Handles equipment procurement, workspace setup, building access, and welcome kit assembly. Ships hardware to remote employees. |
| **IT Administrator** | Provisions all accounts (email, Slack, project management, role-specific tools). Configures hardware. Conducts security orientation. |
| **Finance / Payroll** | Creates the employee record in the payroll system. Collects tax and banking forms. Manages benefits enrollment within the required window. |
| **Assigned Buddy** | Acts as the new hire's informal, day-to-day point of contact for the first 60 days. Not a supervisor -- a peer who helps the new person feel oriented and welcome. |
| **New Employee** | Completes all required paperwork by stated deadlines. Attends scheduled orientation and training sessions. Follows the 30/60/90-day plan. Communicates questions and blockers proactively. |

---

## Prerequisites

Before this process can begin, the following must be true:

- [ ] Signed offer letter has been received from the candidate
- [ ] Start date has been confirmed and is at least 5 business days away
- [ ] Background check has been completed and cleared (if required for the role)
- [ ] Hiring Manager has completed the Role-Specific Training Plan template (HR-001-A)
- [ ] Headcount has been approved and the position is reflected in the budget

## Procedure

### Phase 1: Pre-Start Preparation (5 business days before Day 1)

**Responsible:** Operations Manager (coordinating across IT, Finance, and Office Admin)
**Timeline:** All items must be completed no later than 1 business day before the new hire's start date.

1. **Create the onboarding tracking file.** Copy the Onboarding Tracker template from the HR shared drive (`HR > Templates > Onboarding Tracker Template.xlsx`). Rename it to `Onboarding - [Last Name, First Name] - [Start Date YYYY-MM-DD]`. Save it to `HR > Active Onboarding`. This tracker will be used to confirm every step in this SOP is completed.

2. **Send the welcome email to the new hire.** Use the Welcome Email template (HR-001-B). The email must include:
   - Confirmed start date, start time, and location (street address with parking instructions, or remote login details)
   - What to bring on Day 1: government-issued photo ID and one additional ID document for I-9 verification, voided check or bank routing/account numbers for direct deposit, and any professional licenses or certifications relevant to the role
   - Dress code guidance
   - Name, phone number, and email of their Day 1 point of contact
   - A brief "what to expect" summary so they are not walking in blind

3. **Notify the team.** Post a message in the relevant team Slack channel (or send a group email if Slack is not used by that team). Include the new hire's name, role, start date, and a 2-3 sentence background. Ask team members to be available and welcoming during the first week.

4. **Submit the IT provisioning request.** Open a ticket in the IT request system with the following details:
   - Full legal name and preferred/display name
   - Personal email address (for initial account setup invitations)
   - Department and direct manager
   - Job title (determines standard access permissions and software licenses)
   - Start date
   - On-site or remote (remote hires require laptop shipping; see Step 5)
   - Any role-specific software needs beyond the standard package (confirm with Hiring Manager)

5. **Order equipment and prepare the workspace.**
   - **On-site hires:** Office Administrator reserves a desk or workspace. Ensure it has a monitor, keyboard, mouse, headset, and phone (if applicable). Order a nameplate. Prepare the welcome kit: company swag, notebook, pen, printed one-pager with Wi-Fi credentials and key contacts. Confirm building access badge or key will be ready by Day 1.
   - **Remote hires:** Office Administrator orders a laptop and any required peripherals (monitor, headset, webcam). Ship the package to arrive at least 2 business days before the start date. Include a prepaid return shipping label. Enclose a printed quick-start card with setup instructions and IT support contact information.

6. **Set up payroll and prepare benefits materials.** Finance/Payroll creates the employee record in the payroll system (Gusto). Set the pay schedule, hourly/salary rate per the offer letter, and default withholdings. Prepare the benefits enrollment packet: current health, dental, and vision plan summaries, 401(k) information, and enrollment deadlines. Benefits enrollment must be completed within 30 days of the start date.

7. **Draft the Day 1 agenda.** Operations Manager builds the first-day schedule and sends it to the Hiring Manager for review and adjustment. The schedule should include:

   | Time | Activity | Led By |
   |---|---|---|
   | 9:00 AM | Arrival and welcome | Operations Manager |
   | 9:15 AM | HR paperwork and compliance forms | Operations Manager |
   | 10:00 AM | Benefits overview | Finance / Payroll |
   | 10:45 AM | IT setup and security orientation | IT Administrator |
   | 11:30 AM | Office tour / virtual workspace tour | Office Administrator or Buddy |
   | 12:00 PM | Team lunch | Assigned Buddy |
   | 1:00 PM | Hiring Manager intro: role, team, and current priorities | Hiring Manager |
   | 2:00 PM | Self-guided setup time (explore tools, read handbook) | New Employee |
   | 3:30 PM | End-of-day check-in | Hiring Manager |

   Adjust times for remote hires and different time zones. Remote Day 1 agendas should include buffer time between video calls.

8. **Assign a buddy.** Hiring Manager selects a buddy -- a team member who has been with the company at least 6 months and is not in a supervisory role over the new hire. Notify the buddy, share the Buddy Guidelines document (HR-001-C), and confirm their availability during the new hire's first week.

9. **Share pre-reading materials.** Send the new hire links or attachments for:
   - The employee handbook (current version)
   - The company org chart
   - The team wiki or knowledge base home page
   - Any "about us" materials that help them understand the business before they start

   Mark these as optional reading -- they should not feel like homework, but they help curious people hit the ground faster.

### Phase 2: Day 1 -- Welcome and Foundation

**Responsible:** Operations Manager, Hiring Manager, IT Administrator
**Timeline:** Full first day.

10. **Greet the new hire.** Operations Manager (or designated point of contact) meets the employee at reception (on-site) or sends a welcome Slack DM and confirms the video call link (remote) at the start of the workday. First impressions set the tone -- be warm, unhurried, and prepared.

11. **Complete employment paperwork.** Walk the new hire through each form, explaining what it is and why it matters. Do not just hand them a stack of paper. Required documents:
    - **W-4** -- Federal income tax withholding election
    - **State tax withholding form** (varies by state; confirm which form applies)
    - **I-9 Employment Eligibility Verification** -- Section 1 must be completed on or before Day 1. Section 2 (employer review of identity documents) must be completed within 3 business days. Do not delay this; late I-9 completion is a compliance violation.
    - **Direct deposit authorization** -- Bank name, routing number, account number
    - **Emergency contact form** -- Name, relationship, phone number for at least one contact
    - **Employee handbook acknowledgment** -- Signed confirmation that the employee has received and will review the handbook
    - **Confidentiality / Non-Disclosure Agreement** (if applicable to the role)
    - **At-will employment acknowledgment** (if applicable in your state)
    - **Photo / media release** (if your company uses employee photos in marketing -- this is optional and refusal should have zero consequences)

12. **Conduct benefits enrollment overview.** Finance/Payroll walks through available plans: health, dental, vision, life insurance, disability, and retirement (401k or equivalent). Explain the enrollment deadline (30 days from start date). Provide plan comparison documents. Answer questions, but clarify that detailed coverage questions should go to the benefits provider contact listed on the summary sheet. If the employee needs time to review options with a spouse or family, that is expected -- they do not need to decide on Day 1.

13. **Complete IT setup and security orientation.** IT Administrator:
    - Hands off or confirms receipt of laptop and peripherals
    - Walks the employee through account activation: email (Google Workspace or Microsoft 365), Slack, project management tool (Asana, Monday.com, etc.), file storage (Google Drive, Dropbox, SharePoint), and any role-specific applications
    - Sets up two-factor authentication on all required systems
    - Confirms the employee can access the VPN (if applicable)
    - Reviews the Acceptable Use Policy and Information Security Policy
    - Confirms the employee has signed both policies
    - Provides IT support contact info: how to submit a help ticket, who to call for urgent issues, expected response times

14. **Conduct the office/workspace tour.**
    - **On-site:** Walk through the office -- restrooms, kitchen, conference rooms, emergency exits, supply closet, printer/copier, and any restricted areas. Introduce the employee to people they pass.
    - **Remote:** Walk through the virtual workspace -- Slack channel guide (which channels to join, which are optional), shared drive structure, how to find documents, how to book meetings, and the team calendar.

15. **Team lunch.** The assigned buddy takes the new hire to lunch (on-site: company covers the cost, up to $35 per person; remote: company sends a meal delivery gift card in advance). This is deliberately informal. No agenda, no work talk unless the new hire initiates it.

16. **Hiring Manager role introduction (60 minutes).** Cover:
    - Team mission: what the team exists to accomplish and how it fits into the company's goals
    - Team structure: who reports to whom, who handles what, how the team communicates day-to-day
    - Current projects and priorities: what is active right now, what is coming soon
    - The new hire's role: specific responsibilities, first assignments, what success looks like in the first 30 days
    - Communication norms: when to use Slack vs. email vs. a meeting, response time expectations, how to flag something urgent
    - Introduction to the Role-Specific Training Plan (HR-001-A) and the 30/60/90-day timeline

17. **End-of-day check-in (15 minutes).** Hiring Manager asks:
    - How are you feeling about everything so far?
    - Is anything confusing or missing?
    - Do you have everything you need to start tomorrow?
    - Confirm the schedule for Day 2.

18. **Update the Onboarding Tracker.** Operations Manager checks off all Day 1 items. Flag anything incomplete and schedule follow-up for the next business day.

---

### Phase 3: First Week (Days 2-5)

**Responsible:** Hiring Manager and Assigned Buddy
**Timeline:** Complete by end of Day 5.

19. **Hold daily 15-minute standups with the Hiring Manager (Week 1 only).** These are quick, informal check-ins -- not status reports. Ask: What did you work on yesterday? What is on your plate today? Is anything blocking you? These replace the weekly 1:1 for the first week only.

20. **Begin role-specific training.** Work through the Role-Specific Training Plan (HR-001-A). This typically includes:
    - Detailed walkthroughs of systems and tools used daily in the role
    - Shadowing sessions: the new hire observes a teammate performing key tasks
    - Reverse shadowing: the teammate observes the new hire attempting the task and provides coaching
    - Review of SOPs, process maps, and documentation relevant to their function
    - Introduction to current client accounts or projects they will be supporting

21. **Complete all mandatory compliance training.** Operations Manager confirms enrollment in:
    - Security awareness training (annual requirement, must complete within first week)
    - Anti-harassment and discrimination prevention training (state requirements vary; check compliance calendar)
    - Data handling and privacy training (if the role involves access to customer data, financial data, or PII)
    - Safety training (if applicable to the work environment)

    Track completion in the Onboarding Tracker. Follow up on any incomplete modules by end of Day 5.

22. **Schedule introductory 1:1 meetings.** The new hire should meet individually with:
    - Each member of their immediate team (15-20 minutes each)
    - 2-3 people from adjacent teams they will collaborate with regularly (15-20 minutes each)
    - Any key stakeholders identified by the Hiring Manager

    These are informal "get to know you" conversations. Suggest the new hire ask: What do you do? How will we work together? What should I know that is not written down anywhere?

23. **Review company policies and day-to-day logistics.** Operations Manager (or Hiring Manager) covers:
    - PTO policy: how to request time off, blackout periods, how much PTO they start with
    - Expense reporting: how to submit expenses, what requires pre-approval, reimbursement timelines
    - Remote work policy: expectations for availability, communication, and workspace setup
    - Performance review cycle: when reviews happen, what the process looks like, how compensation decisions are made
    - Professional development: any available budget for courses, conferences, or certifications

24. **Buddy mid-week check-in.** Buddy has a casual 15-20 minute conversation with the new hire. Focus on the informal stuff: How are you settling in? Have you found the coffee? Do you know where to find the things you need? Is anyone particularly helpful to connect with? Report any concerns or blockers to the Hiring Manager.

25. **Hiring Manager end-of-week check-in (30 minutes).** Cover:
    - How the first week went overall
    - Progress against the training plan
    - Questions that have accumulated
    - Whether the new hire has all the tools, access, and information they need
    - Preview of what Week 2 will look like
    - Set up the recurring weekly 1:1 (30 minutes, same day/time each week going forward)

---

### Phase 4: First Month (Days 8-30)

**Responsible:** Hiring Manager
**Timeline:** Ongoing through Day 30, with a formal check-in during Days 25-30.

26. **Assign the first real project or deliverable.** This should be a meaningful but achievable task with clear deliverables and a defined deadline. It gives the new hire something concrete to work toward and demonstrates trust. Avoid busywork.

27. **Continue weekly 1:1 meetings with the Hiring Manager.** These should cover:
    - Progress on current assignments
    - Blockers or areas of confusion
    - Ongoing training needs
    - Relationship-building with the team
    - Feedback -- both directions. Ask the new hire what the company could do better.

28. **Complete all remaining role-specific training modules.** By Day 30, the new hire should have completed every item on the Role-Specific Training Plan (HR-001-A). If they have not, document the gaps and extend the timeline with a revised plan.

29. **Conduct the 30-day check-in meeting (formal).** Hiring Manager schedules 45 minutes. This is a two-way conversation, not a performance review. Use the 30-Day Check-In template (HR-001-D). Cover:
    - How the new hire feels about their role, the team, and the company
    - What is going well and where they feel confident
    - What is confusing, frustrating, or unclear
    - Progress against the training plan -- any gaps to address?
    - Are they getting enough support from their buddy, their manager, and the team?
    - Feedback for the company: what could we improve about onboarding?
    - Set clear, measurable goals for Days 31-60

30. **Operations Manager sends the 30-Day Onboarding Survey.** Use the survey template (HR-001-E). This is anonymous and goes directly to Operations. The survey collects structured feedback on the onboarding experience. Review responses within 5 business days. Flag any systemic issues (not individual complaints) to the relevant department for process improvement.

31. **Verify all compliance items are complete.** Operations Manager audits the Onboarding Tracker and confirms:
    - I-9 is complete, both sections, and filed
    - All federal and state tax forms are submitted and processed
    - Benefits enrollment is finalized or formally waived in writing
    - Employee handbook acknowledgment is signed and filed
    - All mandatory training modules are completed and certificates (if applicable) are filed
    - Confidentiality agreement is signed and filed

    If any item is incomplete, escalate immediately. Do not wait until the 90-day review.

---

### Phase 5: Days 31-60

**Responsible:** Hiring Manager
**Timeline:** Ongoing through Day 60, with a formal check-in during Days 55-60.

32. **Increase responsibility and independence.** The new hire should be taking ownership of tasks and small projects with decreasing oversight. The Hiring Manager shifts from directing to coaching.

33. **Transition from weekly to bi-weekly 1:1s (if appropriate).** If the new hire is performing well and feeling settled, move to bi-weekly 1:1 meetings. If there are concerns, keep the weekly cadence.

34. **Conduct the 60-day check-in meeting.** Hiring Manager schedules 30-45 minutes. Focus on:
    - Performance trajectory: are they meeting expectations for this stage of onboarding?
    - Integration with the team: are working relationships healthy and productive?
    - Progress on the goals set at the 30-day check-in
    - Set goals for Days 61-90
    - Identify any concerns early. If there is a potential performance issue, do not wait until the 90-day review to raise it. Start the conversation now and document it.

35. **Buddy relationship winds down.** The assigned buddy has one final formal check-in with the new hire. After Day 60, the formal buddy relationship ends. The connection should remain as an ongoing resource, but structured check-ins stop.

---

### Phase 6: 90-Day Review and Onboarding Close

**Responsible:** Hiring Manager and Operations Manager
**Timeline:** Scheduled during Days 85-90.

36. **Hiring Manager conducts the 90-day performance review.** This is the first formal evaluation. Schedule 60 minutes. Use the 90-Day Review template (HR-001-F). Cover:
    - Performance against the goals set at the 30 and 60-day milestones
    - Quality of work, attention to detail, and follow-through
    - Initiative and problem-solving ability
    - Cultural fit and collaboration with the team
    - Areas of strength (be specific -- cite examples)
    - Areas for development (be specific and constructive -- provide a path forward)
    - Goals and professional development plan for the next quarter
    - **Employment decision:** Confirm continued employment, or if necessary, initiate a performance improvement plan (SOP-HR-004) or begin the separation process (SOP-HR-005). This decision should never be a surprise if the 30 and 60-day conversations were handled properly.

37. **Close the onboarding file.** Operations Manager:
    - Marks the Onboarding Tracker as complete
    - Moves it to `HR > Completed Onboarding > [Year]`
    - Ensures all documents are filed in the employee's personnel folder (digital or physical)
    - Removes the employee from the "Active Onboarding" dashboard or tracking system

38. **Send the 90-Day Onboarding Survey (HR-001-G).** This final survey captures the complete onboarding experience now that the new hire has full perspective. Results are reviewed quarterly by the Operations Manager to identify trends and drive improvements to this SOP.

39. **Transition to the standard performance management cycle.** The employee is now fully onboarded. Their next formal review will follow the company's regular performance review schedule (typically annual, with semi-annual check-ins). The Hiring Manager confirms the employee is enrolled in the correct review cycle.

## Exceptions

| Exception | Condition | Modified Procedure | Approved By |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Expedited onboarding** | Start date is fewer than 5 business days away, approved by Managing Director due to urgent business need | Compress Phase 1 into available days. Prioritize IT provisioning and equipment above all else. HR paperwork may extend into Day 2. Benefits overview may move to Day 2 or 3. | Managing Director |
| **Remote hire in a different time zone** | Employee is 3+ hours offset from the main office | Adjust the Day 1 schedule to align with overlapping core hours (10:00 AM - 2:00 PM ET). Ship equipment 5 business days early instead of 2. Schedule async-friendly activities for non-overlapping hours. | Operations Manager |
| **Rehire (returning employee)** | Employee left the company in good standing within the last 12 months | Skip general orientation items already completed (office tour, company overview, security awareness training if certificate is still valid). Focus on what has changed since their departure: new tools, new team members, updated policies. A new I-9 is still required by law. | Operations Manager |
| **Senior / executive hire** | Director level or above | Add meetings with all department heads during Week 1. Managing Director conducts the Day 1 welcome and role introduction personally. Include a board or leadership team introduction during the first 30 days. The 90-day review is conducted jointly by the Managing Director and the Hiring Manager. | Managing Director |
| **Employee with accommodations** | New hire has disclosed a disability or requested workplace accommodations | Operations Manager coordinates with the employee (and legal counsel if needed) to ensure accommodations are in place before Day 1. This may include modified equipment, adjusted schedules, assistive technology, or workspace modifications. Document accommodations in a separate confidential file, not in the general onboarding tracker. | Operations Manager |

## Definitions & Acronyms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **I-9** | Employment Eligibility Verification form required by U.S. federal law (USCIS Form I-9). Section 1 is completed by the employee; Section 2 is completed by the employer after reviewing identity documents. Must be completed within 3 business days of the start date. |
| **W-4** | IRS form used by employees to indicate their federal income tax withholding preferences. |
| **PTO** | Paid Time Off -- the company's combined vacation, personal, and sick leave bank. |
| **Buddy** | A peer-level team member assigned to help the new hire acclimate during their first 60 days. The buddy is not a supervisor and does not evaluate performance. |
| **Role-Specific Training Plan** | A document (template HR-001-A) prepared by the Hiring Manager that outlines the specific skills, systems, and knowledge the new hire must acquire, along with a timeline and success criteria. |
| **PII** | Personally Identifiable Information -- any data that could identify a specific individual (name, SSN, address, etc.). |
| **DSO** | Days Sales Outstanding -- a measure of how quickly a company collects payment after a sale. Referenced in related finance SOPs. |
